# Good luck Task Platform Map

Updated: 2026-06-12

Purpose: choose small, low-risk task platforms for Good luck. The current priority is not big grants. The current priority is getting replies, public proof, and small paid tasks.

## Current Payment Reality

- USDT / USDC style crypto payments: usable, but only public receive addresses should be shared.
- Wise: usable for bank-transfer-friendly clients.
- Payoneer: pending verification. Do not open Payoneer on the computer. Use only the phone with the Macau SIM network if Payoneer must be checked.
- PayPal: paused because the old account is permanently limited.

## Best Fit Now

### 1. Superteam Earn

Link: https://superteam.fun/earn/

Why it fits:

- It has Web3 bounties, projects, jobs, and grants.
- Many rewards are in USDC, SOL, USDG, or similar crypto-style payments.
- It has content, design, development, and other categories.

What to look for:

- Content tasks
- Research tasks
- Product explanation tasks
- Beginner guide tasks
- UX feedback tasks that do not require real deposits or wallet signing

What to avoid:

- Tasks that require depositing funds.
- Tasks that require wallet signing before we understand the risk.
- Tasks that require public video if the user is not comfortable.
- Regional-only tasks outside the user's real region.
- Winner-takes-all tasks with many submissions unless we can make a strong entry quickly.

Current judgment: best first non-GitHub platform.

### 2. Gitcoin Bounties

Link: https://gitcoin.co/mechanisms/bounties

Why it fits:

- Bounties are task-based: someone defines work and reward, contributors complete the task.
- It is more aligned with open-source and Web3 public-good work.

What to look for:

- Documentation fixes
- Demo apps
- Short guides
- Simple issue-based work

What to avoid:

- Security bounties.
- Anything that needs private keys, seed phrases, or real money.
- Large engineering tasks before we have more proof.

Current judgment: useful, but probably less beginner-friendly than Superteam.

### 3. Dework

Link: https://dework.xyz/

Why it fits:

- It is a Web3 task board for DAOs and decentralized teams.
- It supports bounty workflows and token payments.

What to look for:

- Community documentation
- Translation or localization
- Notion/GitBook cleanup
- Product feedback

What to avoid:

- DAO tasks that require Discord reputation first.
- Tasks that are unclear about payment or selection.
- Tasks that require wallet connection before reading details.

Current judgment: useful second wave after Superteam.

## Watch Later

### DoraHacks

Link: https://dorahacks.io/

Why watch:

- Good for hackathons and demos.
- Codex can help build proof-of-concept projects.

Why not first:

- Usually heavier than a 30-120 minute task.
- More competition.
- Less likely to produce quick small income.

### Contra / Upwork / Fiverr / Freelancer / PeoplePerHour

Why watch:

- Real freelance marketplaces.
- Good once we have 3-5 public proof samples.

Why not first:

- New accounts start with low trust.
- Competition is high.
- Payment setup may depend on PayPal, Payoneer, bank transfer, or platform approval.
- Payoneer is still pending and PayPal is paused.

## Not Suitable Now

### UserTesting / Userlytics and similar UX testing platforms

Why not now:

- They are conceptually suitable because the work is website/app testing.
- But payment often depends on PayPal.
- Since PayPal is paused, this is not a good first route.

Current judgment: revisit only if PayPal or another supported payout method becomes usable.

## Recommended Next Move

- Content
- Other
- Design

Search terms:

- documentation
- guide
- beginner
- feedback
- review

First acceptable task should meet all rules:

- No deposit
- No wallet signing
- No private key
